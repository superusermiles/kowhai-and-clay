# Kōwhai & Clay — Project Brief

## Business Overview
Kōwhai & Clay is a boutique ceramic studio based in Raglan, Aotearoa New Zealand. The brand creates small-batch tableware, sculptural vessels, and seasonal home objects inspired by native flora, black-sand coastlines, and sun-warmed earth tones. Products are wheel-thrown and hand-finished in short production runs, with a mix of made-to-order pieces and limited seasonal drops.

The business positions itself between artisan craft and refined interior design. It is not a hobbyist pottery brand. It feels collected, grounded, and quietly premium.

## Offer
- Handmade ceramic dinnerware
- Sculptural vases and planters
- Seasonal object collections
- Custom hospitality pieces for boutique cafés and accommodation brands
- Small in-studio workshops
- Online shop with collection storytelling

## Goals
1. Establish a strong premium visual identity.
2. Make the studio feel tactile, calm, and editorial.
3. Drive enquiries for custom commissions and workshop bookings.
4. Support future e-commerce expansion with collection-led storytelling.
5. Present the founder as an experienced maker with a clear point of view.

## Target Audience
### Primary
- Design-conscious women and couples aged 28–50
- New Zealand homeowners, stylists, and boutique hospitality operators
- Shoppers who value handmade objects, natural materials, and slower living

### Secondary
- Visitors to Raglan looking for workshops or meaningful local gifts
- Interior designers sourcing ceramics for residential projects
- Boutique retailers interested in stocking limited-run collections

## Brand Personality
- Grounded
- Warm
- Refined
- Tactile
- Seasonal
- Quietly confident

The brand should feel like a well-styled interior editorial rather than a loud online store.

## Brand Voice
Use clear, measured language. Short sentences. Sensory wording is good when restrained. Avoid trendy wellness clichés and overly romantic copy. Sound thoughtful, local, and assured.

## Key Messages
- Handmade in Raglan, Aotearoa
- Small-batch ceramics with natural variation
- Functional objects designed to be used daily
- Crafted for calm homes, thoughtful tables, and collected spaces
- Bespoke work available for hospitality and design partners

## Conversion Priorities
1. Commission enquiry

3. Collection/shop browsing

## Content Direction
Photography should feel cohesive and softly editorial:
- Natural light
- Honest textures
- Hands, shelves, tablescapes, glazing process
- Warm neutrals with muted botanical accents
- A consistent balance of product detail and spatial context

## Localisation
- NZ English
- NZD references where relevant
- Raglan, Waikato as the location anchor

## Practical Notes
- Build for Next.js static export.
- Use direct Unsplash image URLs in plain img tags.
- Keep layouts image-led, not text-heavy.
- Ensure the site can later support a simple e-commerce layer without redesigning the brand system.
